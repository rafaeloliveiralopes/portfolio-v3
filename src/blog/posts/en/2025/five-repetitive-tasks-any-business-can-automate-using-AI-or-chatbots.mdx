# 5 repetitive tasks any company can automate using AI or a chatbot

Companies of all sizes carry out a variety of repetitive operational tasks every day, from administrative activities to marketing and sales actions. The good news is that Artificial Intelligence (AI) can automate many of these routines, freeing teams up for work with greater strategic value.

Below, we’ll explore five common, repetitive tasks in administration, marketing, and sales that can be automated with AI. For each one, we discuss the day-to-day work involved, how AI helps optimize or execute the task, the measurable benefits achieved (time, cost, revenue, or scale), and an illustrative example of how it’s applied.

## 1. Sending Emails and Follow-ups

In the daily lives of people working in marketing and sales, it’s common to spend hours sending bulk emails, confirmations, payment reminders, or follow-ups to potential customers. These communications usually follow standardized scripts (newsletter, outreach, post-meeting follow-up) and need to be sent at the right times to be effective. Manually, this consumes valuable team time and is prone to errors or oversights.

However, automation tools and AI can handle the entire flow of sending emails and follow-up messages in a personalized way. Through machine learning and NLP, AI can segment the contact base, personalize each message’s content (such as inserting the customer’s name and products of interest), and schedule delivery at the best time for each segment.

Chatbots and virtual assistants can also send instant automated responses triggered by certain actions. For example, when a lead downloads an asset, the system sends a follow-up email.

Generative AI models can even draft convincing email copies from just a few key points provided by the salesperson. As a result, a large portion of routine customer correspondence can happen without direct human intervention.

In terms of benefits, automating emails and follow-ups clearly boosts efficiency and results. And the most obvious is time savings. Sellers who use AI to automate manual tasks like sending messages can save an average of 2 hours and 15 minutes per day (according to a HubSpot study), freeing up that time for strategic negotiations and personal customer contact.

Unlike humans, AI doesn’t “forget” to reply to a prospect or do a follow-up. This eliminates gaps in opportunity nurturing and ensures that every stage of the sales funnel is covered on time.

Just to illustrate, imagine a small services company dealing with dozens of leads per week. Without AI, the salesperson has to remember to manually send an email to each contact after a meeting and send additional follow-ups days later, which can lead to oversights or delays.

On the other hand, by implementing an email marketing automation solution, the company can set up automated sequences immediately after the meeting. The lead will receive a personalized thank-you email; two days later, a message with additional materials; a week later, a decision reminder.

Plus, the content of each email can be generated by AI and tailored based on the lead’s profile. The result is that 100% of leads receive proper follow-up without depending on the salesperson’s memory, and response rates tend to increase significantly.

In short, smartly automating email sends and follow-ups with AI allows you and your team to direct time and energy toward other strategic actions for your business.

## 2. Customer Support for Repetitive Topics

Answering customers’ frequently asked questions, whether about order status, return policies, pricing, or product or service features, is a daily, tiring task for many companies. Support and sales teams spend countless hours handling calls or messages with the same recurring questions. This traditional one-to-one service, besides being costly, can’t always offer quick answers outside business hours.

The good news is that this repetitive task can be optimized with intelligent chatbots to handle initial customer support. Integrated into websites, apps, or WhatsApp, these virtual assistants can understand natural language and instantly answer customers’ most common questions.

The chatbot’s knowledge base can be trained on the company’s FAQs, product manuals, and interaction history. So when a customer asks “What’s the delivery time?” or “How do I make a return?”, the chatbot provides an immediate answer according to company policies.

For example, consider an online store that receives dozens of daily questions about product details, order tracking, and policies. With an AI chatbot integrated into the website and WhatsApp, customers get immediate answers: if they ask “Has my order shipped yet?”, the bot checks the system and shares the status; if they ask “What’s the right size for me?”, the bot can offer guidance based on the measurements provided.

Overnight or on weekends, the assistant stays active helping customers. Only unanticipated issues (e.g., a specific payment problem) get escalated to a human agent on the next business day. With that, the store starts resolving roughly 80% of common questions automatically and maintains continuous support every day of the week at any hour.

The result is that investing in automating this service keeps your customers happy with the quick response, while you and your team, relieved of repetitive questions, can focus more attention on delicate cases that require a human action or decision.

## 3. Scheduling Meetings and Appointments

Booking meetings, interviews, or visits is a time-consuming task in administrative routines. Often, it takes multiple email exchanges or calls just to find a time that works for everyone involved.

In smaller companies, this task is usually done by the owner or an assistant, while in larger companies, assistants manage executives’ and teams’ calendars. It’s repetitive, mechanical work that involves checking everyone’s calendars, proposing time slots, adjusting based on responses, and sometimes rescheduling when conflicts arise.

But what if we could streamline this task? Smart calendar assistants, such as AI-enabled calendars or automated scheduling tools, can take over this role. These solutions access participants’ digital calendars and, using algorithms, automatically identify time slots available to all.

Examples of how this works:

1. **Smart Invites**: Tools can send email invites with preselected time options. The organizer indicates available windows, and the participant picks with a single click, eliminating manual back-and-forth.
2. **Conversational AI**: You can copy a virtual agent on an email, and it will “negotiate” the time with the client or colleague, autonomously proposing and confirming the meeting. For example, just tell the AI assistant: “Schedule a 30-minute meeting with João for next Monday.”
3. **Travel Logistics**: For offsite appointments, some agents even coordinate travel logistics, booking flights, hotels, and transportation according to the parameters you provide.

### Here’s a practical example

A consulting firm adopted a smart scheduling agent integrated with the consultants’ Google Calendar. When a client requested a meeting, instead of exchanging several emails asking “When are you available?”, the consultant simply triggered the assistant.

The agent checked both calendars and replied by proposing an available time the following week. The client confirmed with one click and automatically received an invite with a Zoom link, which was added to both calendars.

If the client needed to reschedule, they just informed the assistant, which found a new option. At the end of the month, the consultants realized they had saved countless hours previously spent on scheduling logistics. That time was invested in prospecting new clients and researching solutions, far more productive activities.

## 4. Report Generation and Data Analysis

Companies depend on periodic reports such as financial statements, sales reports, marketing metrics, and inventory. Traditionally, producing these reports involves extracting data from different systems (ERP, spreadsheets, CRM), consolidating them in a document, creating charts, and highlighting insights. It’s a repetitive task and prone to errors, especially with large data volumes.

AI techniques and robotic process automation (RPA) make it possible to automate much of the reporting cycle. Smart scripts automatically pull data from multiple sources, apply formulas, and update dashboards in real time. AI-powered tools generate visualizations and insights with minimal human intervention.

AI not only aggregates the data but also quickly points out patterns, anomalies, and possible causes. This allows managers to receive ready-made analyses that are automatically updated, focusing on the critical points.

Automating report generation brings several benefits. What used to take days can now be obtained in minutes. Reports that were monthly can become weekly or even daily, thanks to continuously updated data. Real-time information improves decision-making.

Copy-and-paste errors or incorrect calculations are eliminated, since AI follows consistent rules, ensuring accuracy and reliability. Algorithms detect trends and correlations that might go unnoticed, such as identifying that a region had a sales dip due to a local holiday.

Analysts and managers save hours previously spent assembling reports. In many sales teams, only 35% of time is dedicated to selling, while the rest goes to administrative tasks. By automating these tasks, the team gets more time for commercial activities. Uniformly generated reports make it easier to compare periods and business units, maintaining methodological consistency.

A practical example illustrates these benefits well. A retail company used to manually produce a monthly store-by-store sales performance report. The process took two full workdays. After adopting an AI-powered automated dashboard, sales data from each branch is integrated in real time. At month’s end, the manager accesses the online panel and generates a PDF with updated charts in seconds.

Generative AI highlights important insights, such as: “Store X showed an atypical 30% growth driven by items in the Electronics category.” With that, the company saves around 16 hours of human work per month and gets richer, immediate analyses. Analysts now interpret results and propose actions instead of compiling data. Report automation increased the frequency of monitoring and gave the team more agility to react to trends.

## 5. Document Processing and Data Entry

Every company, regardless of size or industry, deals daily with large volumes of standardized documents: invoices, vendor bills, customer records, internal forms, and even résumés. Traditionally, processing these documents involves manual tasks: typing data into systems, checking fields, filing records. These are repetitive activities, prone to human error, and they consume valuable employee time that could be invested in strategic decisions, service, or innovation.

But what if you could turn this operational bottleneck into an agile, accurate, and almost fully automated process?

### From Manual Typing to Artificial Intelligence

Consider the classic example of accounts payable and receivable: someone needs to open each invoice, identify amounts, due dates, vendor codes, and enter everything into the finance system. Likewise, moving data from a spreadsheet to a CRM or updating profiles requires meticulous attention, and even then, errors happen.

The good news? These tasks can already be automated with high accuracy thanks to a combination of Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and Intelligent Document Processing (IDP), technologies powered by artificial intelligence.

Modern intelligent document processing solutions don’t just “read” PDFs or scanned images, they understand context. When analyzing an invoice, for example, the software automatically identifies:

* Vendor name and CNPJ
* Items purchased and their respective amounts
* Taxes and fiscal codes (such as CFOP)
* Due date and payment terms

And the best part: it populates this data directly into your ERP or accounting system without human intervention. Thanks to machine learning, AI learns from different layouts, even if each vendor sends an invoice in a distinct format. Over time, accuracy only increases.

### Beyond OCR: End-to-End Intelligent Automation

In fully digital environments where data already lives in systems, Robotic Process Automation (RPA) comes into play. Smart scripts simulate human actions: they navigate between screens, copy information, and fill in fields across different platforms, continuously and without breaks.

In short, any task like “take data from A and put it in B” or “read a structured document and interpret its fields” is a perfect candidate for optimization and automation using artificial intelligence.

### Tangible Benefits of Document Automation

1. **Unprecedented Operational Speed**
   An AI system processes in seconds what would take hours or days for a human team. This speeds up financial cycles, profile updates, and partner responses, making the company more agile and competitive.

2. **Near-Perfect Accuracy**
   Typing errors, digit swaps, or omissions are virtually eliminated. This reduces rework, avoids incorrect payments, and ensures data integrity.

3. **Lower Operational Costs**
   Automating doesn’t mean eliminating jobs, but rather redirecting talent. Employees move away from repetitive tasks to focus on analysis, relationships, and continuous improvement. In addition, faster processing avoids late fees and improves cash flow.

4. **Natural Scalability**
   If your company grows and document volume doubles, triples, or quadruples, AI scales with you, requiring only infrastructure tweaks. Manual processes, on the other hand, would require proportional hiring, training, and carry a higher risk of inconsistency.

5. **Traceability and Compliance**
   Every action is recorded: who processed it, when, what data was extracted, and whether there was human intervention. This streamlines audits, strengthens governance, and enables proactive fraud or anomaly detection.

If you’re still manually typing invoices, spreadsheets, or résumés, it’s time to rethink your operational flow. The technology is mature, accessible, and ready to deliver real results from day one of implementation.

### 📑 **Glossary of Technical Terms and Expressions**

If you’re not familiar with some of the words used in this article, here’s a brief glossary to help you understand the most important concepts in a simple, straightforward way.

**Chatbot:** A computer program that simulates human conversation, used for customer support and automated interactions.

**Process Automation:** The use of technology to perform repetitive tasks without human intervention, connecting different systems and tools.

**No-code/Low-code:** Development approaches that allow you to create applications and automations with little or no code, making them accessible to non-technical users.

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** A system that helps manage customer relationships by storing information and interactions.

**API (Application Programming Interface):** A set of rules that allows different software systems to communicate with each other.

**AI Agent:** A system that uses artificial intelligence to understand commands, make decisions, and execute actions autonomously based on data and context.

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** A technique used in AI that combines searching information in databases with text generation, ensuring more accurate and up-to-date answers.

**Retainer (Ongoing Support Contract):** A service model in which the client pays monthly to maintain updates, technical support, and continuous adjustments to the implemented automation or chatbot.

**Scalability:** A system’s ability to handle increasing users or tasks without losing performance.
